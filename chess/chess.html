It Is 's Turn.

Game Rules

  • Each player starts with 16 pieces: 1 king, 1 queen, 2 rooks, 2 knights, 2 bishops, and 8 pawns.
  • The objective is to checkmate the opponent's king by placing it under attack in such a way that it cannot escape capture.
  • Players take turns moving one piece at a time.
  • Players can promote a pawn to any other piece (except a king) upon reaching the opponent's back rank.
  • Pieces move in specific ways: pawns forward, rooks horizontally/vertically, knights in an "L" shape, bishops diagonally, queen in any direction, and king one square in any direction.
  • A player can capture an opponent's piece by landing on its square.
  • The game can end in checkmate, stalemate, or draw by agreement.
